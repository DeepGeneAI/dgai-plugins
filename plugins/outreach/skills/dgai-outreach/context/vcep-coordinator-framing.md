# VCEP coordinator framing

## Who they are

ClinGen VCEP and GCEP members are volunteer researchers — clinicians, lab directors, and academic scientists who classify genetic variants in their spare time alongside their primary jobs. 2,950 individuals across 136 panels, 911 in leadership roles (chairs, co-chairs, coordinators).

**Coordinators vs. chairs:** Coordinators administer the panel — scheduling, tracking, managing submissions. That is their job. Chairs are typically PIs with competing demands. Coordinators are often more responsive to cold outreach for this reason.

**Panel stages:** Panels move through formation → rules development → piloting → active variant curation. Stage determines which offer fits. A panel in active curation has a different problem than one still writing its specifications.

## What they care about

- Literature review is the biggest time sink. Confirmed by Amanda Thomas-Wilson (New York Genome Center) and Adam Chamberlain (commercial lab). Finding, reading, and extracting evidence from papers is the bottleneck.
- VUS backlog. Most panels have more Uncertain Significance variants than they have bandwidth to resolve.
- ClinVar conflicts. Disagreements between their Expert Panel record and other submitters require active monitoring and response.
- The V4 Bayesian framework transition. Expected mid-2026. The most significant retooling of variant classification in years. Panels that engage with Qorus now have input into how V4 support is built.

## How to talk to them

- Peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-customer. These are scientists. Write as one scientist to another, not as a founder pitching.
- No product marketing language. "Qorus is a platform that..." reads as a sales pitch. Lead with what the report delivers for their specific gene, not what the software does.
- Respect volunteer time. VCEP work is unpaid. Acknowledge this — briefly, once — when it adds honesty. Don't overdo it.
- Gene name personalization is the single highest-leverage step. A recipient who sees their gene named in the first email responds at significantly higher rates than one who receives generic outreach.
- Warm introductions are the highest-yield path in this community. It is tightly networked. A specific named intro request lands better than any cold email.

## What not to say

- "Your work with [Panel Name] is exactly the kind of perspective I'm looking for." Generic, reads as a template.
- "Who else should I talk to?" Open-ended asks have not worked. Specific named requests work.
- Anything implying the report replaces curator judgment. Qorus focuses curator attention; expert judgment stays with the panel.
