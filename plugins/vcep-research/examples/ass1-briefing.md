## Scope and phenotype

- **Scope:** ASS1 gene — variant interpretation criteria for argininosuccinate synthetase 1 deficiency (citrullinemia type I), under the ClinGen Urea Cycle Disorders Expert Panel Specifications to the ACMG/AMP guidelines.[^1]
- **Phenotype:** Citrullinemia type I (CTLN1; OMIM #215700) is an autosomal recessive urea cycle disorder caused by ASS1 deficiency; it presents with hyperammonemia and citrulline accumulation, with a spectrum from severe neonatal-onset encephalopathy to mild or asymptomatic forms.[^4]

## Recent ClinVar activity

More than 100 ASS1 variants are documented in ClinVar for citrullinemia type I, submitted by multiple clinical labs.[^5] A 2025 high-throughput functional study of 2,193 ASS1 missense variants found evidence supporting pathogenicity for 547 substitutions, including reclassification potential for 25 variants currently designated VUS in ClinVar — suggesting ongoing active reclassification work.[^6]

## Key literature

1. **Landstrom et al. (2025, preprint)** — High-throughput functional profiling of 2,193 ASS1 missense variants (90% of SNV-accessible substitutions); characterizes epistatic interactions and provides a scored dataset directly applicable to PS3/BS3 evidence.[^6]

2. **Guo X et al. (2023)** — Functional identification of two novel variants and one hypomorphic non-coding variant in ASS1 using western blotting, qPCR, immunofluorescence, and ELISA; validates pathogenicity with functional studies per ACMG criteria.[^7]

3. **Alfadhel M et al. (2023)** — Analysis of ASS1 in ten unrelated Middle Eastern families with citrullinemia type I; identifies rare and novel variants, illustrating the diagnostic value of population-specific variant data.[^8]
