## Scope and phenotype

- **Scope:** OTC gene only — variant interpretation criteria for ornithine transcarbamylase deficiency, per "ClinGen Urea Cycle Disorders Expert Panel Specifications to the ACMG/AMP Variant Interpretation Guidelines for OTC Version 1.0.0."[^1]
- **Phenotype:** Ornithine transcarbamylase (OTC) deficiency is an X-linked disorder of the urea cycle; hemizygous males typically present with neonatal hyperammonemia, while heterozygous females show variable severity ranging from asymptomatic to life-threatening.[^4]

## Recent ClinVar activity

Approximately 643 variants in OTC are recorded in ClinVar for ornithine transcarbamylase deficiency.[^5] Submitters include GeneDx, Eurofins NTD, DASA, Rady Children's Institute for Genomic Medicine, GenMed Metabolism Lab, and OMIM — indicating broad clinical engagement. Variant-level modification counts for the past two years could not be retrieved directly; the ClinVar gene view (see footnote) reflects current classification breakdown by significance and review status. The c.-106C>A promoter variant (RCV000627032) has received recent attention as a recognized cause of late-onset OTC deficiency.[^6]

## Key literature

1. **Lo RS et al. (2023)** — High-throughput functional assay measuring residual activity for 1,570 SNV-accessible OTC missense variants; distinguishes neonatal-onset from late-onset variants and provides a scored dataset directly applicable to PS3/BS3 evidence under the VCEP's criteria.[^7]

2. **Staufner C et al. (2025/2026)** — Biochemical, clinical, and enzymatic characterization of the c.-106C>A OTC promoter variant across multiple families; supports PS4 + PS3 + PP4 + PP1 classification as pathogenic for late-onset OTC deficiency.[^8]

3. **Li X et al. (2024)** — Variant analysis and preimplantation genetic testing (PGT-M) for OTC in a Chinese pedigree; applies ACMG 2015 criteria to novel missense variants using standard computational and functional tools.[^9]
