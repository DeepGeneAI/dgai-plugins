# VCEP Briefing: PAH — Phenylketonuria Expert Panel

**Panel ID:** 50015 · **Type:** VCEP · **ClinGen:** https://clinicalgenome.org/affiliation/50015/
**Prepared:** 2026-06-09 · **Sources:** ClinGen/CSpec, ClinVar, PubMed, OMIM

## Status and stage

- **CSpec status:** Expert Panel Approved, Version 2 (updated December 2024)[^1]
- **Inferred stage:** Active curation — PAH was the pilot gene for the ClinGen Inborn Errors of Metabolism Working Group's ACMG/AMP criteria adaptation; version 2 criteria published December 2024 reflect ongoing refinement. The panel is one of the most active VCEPs with over 3,300 variants in ClinVar.[^2]

## Chairs and coordinator

- **Chair:** William J. Craigen, MD, PhD — Baylor College of Medicine[^3]
- **Coordinator:** Ashley Ratzsch, MS, GC — American College of Medical Genetics and Genomics[^3]

## Scope and phenotype

- **Scope:** PAH gene — variant interpretation criteria for phenylalanine hydroxylase deficiency (phenylketonuria and mild hyperphenylalaninemia), per the ClinGen PAH Expert Panel Specifications to the ACMG/AMP Variant Interpretation Guidelines Version 2.[^1]
- **Phenotype:** Phenylketonuria (PKU; OMIM #261600) is an autosomal recessive disorder caused by PAH deficiency, leading to hyperphenylalaninemia; the phenotypic spectrum ranges from classic PKU with intellectual disability (untreated) to mild hyperphenylalaninemia requiring no treatment.[^4]

## Recent ClinVar activity

PAH has 3,369 reported variants in ClinVar, making it one of the most variant-rich genes in the database.[^5] 33.7% are missense substitutions; approximately 30% of missense variants remain VUS, representing a significant active curation burden.[^5] The PAH VCEP's published interpretations showed 81% concordance with existing ClinVar classifications across 85 curated variants — reflecting both the panel's impact and the scale of reclassification work still underway.[^6] Multiple labs submit actively (GeneDx, Baylor, Emory, and others).

## Key literature

1. **Weaver MA et al. (2019)** — Founding paper describing the ClinGen IEM Working Group's adaptation of ACMG/AMP criteria for PAH; defines PAH-specific rules for functional assay thresholds, allele frequency, and phenotype criteria that underpin the current VCEP spec.[^6]

2. **American College of Medical Genetics and Genomics (2023)** — Evidence-based clinical guideline for PAH deficiency diagnosis and management; strongly endorses genetic testing at birth and provides the clinical framework within which variant classification is applied.[^7]

3. **Tounsi B et al. (2025)** — Genomic profiling of 131 PKU patients; characterizes novel PAH variants and their genotype-treatment implications, illustrating current clinical demand for resolved variant classifications.[^8]

4. **Vásquez-Hernández AL et al. (2024)** — Systematic landscape of PAH variants using in silico structural analysis and phenotypic data; provides a framework for resolving the large VUS backlog using computational evidence.[^9]
