# Example 1 — Warm first-touch, multi-offer

**Scenario:** Fictional coordinator with a warm intro source. Gene-stage not pinnable precisely, so three offers surfaced as a menu.

**Recipient (fictional):** Dr. Sarah Chen, Coordinator, Hereditary Spastic Paraplegia VCEP, Children's Hospital of Philadelphia. Primary gene: SPG4/SPAST. Panel is in active curation (Step 4), known to have a significant VUS backlog and recent conflicting submissions from commercial labs. Warm intro available via Heidi Rehm.

---

SUBJECT: SPG4 Gene Report (Heidi Rehm intro)

Hi Sarah,

Heidi Rehm suggested I reach out. She thought the work we're doing on variant evidence for SPG4 might be useful for where your panel is right now.

We've built a Gene Report for genes in active curation. For SPG4 specifically, it can pull three things: conflicting ClinVar submissions that are likely resolvable with existing literature, variants where evidence may already support a reclassification, and a VUS triage queue ranked by evidence completeness. No call required. A 2-minute survey is all we need: [survey link].

Which, if any, of those would be useful at your current stage? Happy to put together whichever fits best, or all three if that's helpful. Just let me know.

Best,
Bryce

---

- Warm-intro source: Heidi Rehm (named specifically, not generic name-drop)

- Offer path: gene-stage not pinnable to a single row in stage-offer-mapping, surfaced default three (ClinVar discrepancy, reclassification candidates, VUS triage queue)
- Per-paper evidence summaries: held back (no stated literature bottleneck)
