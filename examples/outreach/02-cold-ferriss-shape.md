# Example 2 — Cold first-touch, Ferriss shape

**Scenario:** Fictional chair, no warm intro source. Gene-stage pinnable (building pilot variant list from scratch). Ferriss shape: 3–5 sentences, one specific fact, one small frictionless ask.

**Recipient (fictional):** Dr. Marcus Webb, Chair, Alagille Syndrome VCEP, Baylor College of Medicine. Primary gene: JAG1. Panel recently published their specifications and is now building their pilot variant list, no variants deposited in ClinVar yet under the panel's record. No known mutual connection.

---

SUBJECT: JAG1 pilot list: reclassification candidates

Hi Marcus,

Your panel's JAG1 specifications published last month. For a panel building a pilot variant list from scratch, the most useful starting point is usually reclassification candidates: variants where existing evidence may already support a classification update, so you're not starting from a blank slate.

Do you think that would be useful for JAG1? [survey link], 2 minutes, no call required.

Best,
Bryce
